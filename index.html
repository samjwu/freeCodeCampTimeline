About freeCodeCamp

AKA Free Code Camp, Inc.

A rough timeline of freeCodeCamp

  • October 2014 - freeCodeCamp is launched by Quincy Larson and is incorporated as Free Code Camp, Inc.
  • April 2015 - The original curriculum is created, which focuses on MongoDB, Express.js, AngularJS, and Node.js and is estimated to take 800 hours to complete.
  • January 2016 - The curriculum is updated to rely less on outside material and switch focus from AngularJS to React.js as the front-end library of choice. Some additions to the coursework are made, including a D3.js course, a Sass course, a data visualization certificate, and a back-end certificate. The total time estimate to complete the course is 2,080 hours.
  • May 2016 - A freeCodeCamp survey reveals there are students from over 160 countries.
  • September 2016 - freeCodeCamp announces their "Open Source for Good" initiative, which extends and open sources their nonprofit work to all nonprofits and organizations to use.
  • January 2017 - freeCodeCamp analytics reveals that the platform is used by about 350,000 unique visitors per month.
  • October 2022 - To celebrate freeCodeCamp's 8th birthday, Quincy Larson publishes a tweet announcing free accredited degree programs in mathematics and computer science are in active development.

"freeCodeCamp is my effort to correct the extremely inefficient and circuitous way I learned to code. I'm committing my career and the rest of my life towards making this process as efficient and painless as possible. [...] All those things that made learning to code a nightmare to me are things that we are trying to fix with freeCodeCamp"

-- Quincy Larson
